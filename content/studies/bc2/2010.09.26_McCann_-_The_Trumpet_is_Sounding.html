Let us turn to the book of Amos. I just want to start off here, even though we are not really going to be looking too much at this verse. It says in Amos 3:3-8:

Can two walk together, except they be agreed? Will a lion roar in the forest, when he hath no prey? will a young lion cry out of his den, if he have taken nothing? Can a bird fall in a snare upon the earth, where no gin is for him? shall one take up a snare from the earth, and have taken nothing at all? Shall a trumpet be blown in the city, and the people not be afraid? shall there be evil in a city, and JEHOVAH hath not done it? Surely the Lord JEHOVAH will do nothing, but he revealeth his secret unto his servants the prophets. The lion hath roared, who will not fear? the Lord JEHOVAH hath spoken, who can but prophesy?

I wanted to go here because I have been thinking about trumpets a lot lately. There is a new Family Radio billboard image. Actually, they have different ones; but one of them says, “The Trumpet is Sounding!” It has an illustration of trumpets with the nations’ flags attached to each trumpet. When a billboard is put up like, for example, in Mexico, then they put the Mexican flag right at the forefront. But, basically, the billboards are saying that the trumpet is sounding.

So we do find in Amos 3 that there is language of a trumpet. We read again in Amos 3:6:

Shall a trumpet be blown in the city, and the people not be afraid?…

This is in relation to Amos 3:7, where God says:

Surely the Lord JEHOVAH will do nothing, but he revealeth his secret unto his servants the prophets.

So there is a connection between the blowing of the trumpet and the revealing of information from the Bible, and we know that this verse is telling us of God’s plan. It is not stating it directly, but it is telling us of God’s plan at the end to open the Scriptures and to reveal the things that were sealed up “till the time of the end”; and the trumpet plays a role in this.

When we think about a trumpet in the Bible, it could mean different things depending on the context. In the book of Numbers, for instance, they would blow the trumpet in order to gather the people. The people would muster at the sound of the trumpet. On the other hand, they would blow the trumpet if they were preparing for war, for a battle.

We also know that the trumpet has much to do or much connection with the ending of the world. If we go to Matthew 24:31, it says:

And he shall send his angels with a great sound of a trumpet, and they shall gather together his elect from the four winds, from one end of heaven to the other.

This verse does not stand alone. Look at 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17. It says:

For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel [or chief messenger], and with the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first: Then we which are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air: and so shall we ever be with the Lord.

Here, again, this speaks of the coming of Christ. This is clearly talking about the end of the world because May 21st is the end of the world. It is. Even though there will be five months of time following, basically, that will be it. That will be it because there will be no more salvation, only five months of torment. You are done. If you are left behind, it is over.

Do not worry about the week leading up to May 21st. In the week before May 21st of next year, how many people do you think all over the world are going to be hitting the supermarkets trying to get gallons of water, trying to stock up as if they are expecting an earthquake? Well, there will be an earthquake! However, what would be the sense of doing this?

Believe me; people will be doing this, because the whole world is going to hear about this. But what would be the sense in trying to stock up on supplies so that you have provision for those five months? You would believe it enough, you would believe it just enough to prepare for this in this way on the physical level, but you would not believe it enough to go to God and to cry out to Him and beseech Him for mercy. This does not make any sense.

As soon as the weatherman says that it is going to snow, everybody is running to Home Depot; and yet, here, by this time next year, God will have been forecasting this for several years. I am really expecting this. I am really expecting people to stock up on flashlights and emergency supplies, which tells us that they have been thinking about it. However, what in the world are they thinking? What are they thinking?

So they make it for the whole five months? Will this be anything great? No, because their lives will be over on May 21st. Whoever is left here after God takes His people, their lives are over.

I have been thinking about this because of what someone asked me. He is a believer and he knows that Judgment Day is May 21st, but he has people in his family who are not. Because of this, he was wondering if he should go and buy supplies in order to physically prepare them for this period of time.

As fathers and mothers today, we take care of our families and provide for them. We want them to have food and supplies. However, we are under no obligation to prepare those who are going to be judged for the judgment. We have no obligation to stock up on food or water or anything else. All of this will be in God’s hands once May 21st comes.

For the fathers and mothers who believe these things, we have all kinds of obligations towards our families right now on the spiritual level as well as on the physical level. However, we have none after May 21st; because then, it is in God’s hands.

So in 1 Thessalonians 4, we see that Christ is coming with the sound of a trumpet. In 1 Corinthians 15:51-52, it says:

Behold, I show you a mystery; We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed, In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed.

He is speaking of a trumpet blast. God is saying that on that day, there will be a great sound of a trumpet, which will be the last trumpet. This is the last trumpet that the Bible speaks of, and yet we know now that the trumpet is sounding. The trumpet is sounding today, and yet it is not the end of the world. It is not here yet, but the trumpet is sounding.

For instance, go to Ezekiel 33. It says in Ezekiel 33:2-3:

Son of man, speak to the children of thy people, and say unto them, When I bring the sword upon a land, if the people of the land take a man of their coasts, and set him for their watchman: If when he seeth the sword come upon the land, he blow the trumpet, and warn the people;

Then it goes on to discuss this a little more.

So the watchman sees the sword coming. He has a trumpet with him and he blows the trumpet. This warns the people, and this is how it has been throughout history. According to the tune that was played, the trumpet could be sounding a warning or it could be to gather the people together, and so forth. There were different types of trumpet blasts, different sounds that it could give.

There was also one that warned the people of an approaching enemy. In the Old Testament days, they would have a watchman in the tower who would keep a lookout for the city. When he saw an enemy army advancing, he would blow on the trumpet and the people of the city would be warned.

Well, here, it is telling us what the watchman does. So, first of all, who is a watchman?

Look at Habakkuk 2. Habakkuk 2:1 says:

I will stand upon my watch, and set me upon the tower, and will watch to see what he will say unto me, and what I shall answer when I am reproved.

So this watchman is set in a tower, but he is waiting to see what God will say to him. It is not like he is looking for anything visible to his eye. He is waiting for a sound. He is waiting for information to come from God.

What did Jesus say again and again and again? He said to “watch”; “watch therefore.” In doing this, He was setting the watchman up. He was telling people, His followers, “You are a watchman, and you are a watchman, and you are a watchman. You are to watch for the sword to come. When you see it coming, you are to blow on the trumpet to warn the people.”

However, we know that this cannot be literal. We are not to go up on the top of our roof and keep a lookout. There would be no point in doing this. It is not an earthly army that we are looking for. We are not looking in the sky for any kind of visible evidence of Christ’s return.

The only place to keep watch is in the Bible; and here in Habakkuk after speaking of the watchman on the watchtower who is waiting to hear what God will say to him, it says in Habakkuk 2:2-3:

And JEHOVAH answered me, and said, Write the vision, and make it plain upon tables, that he may run that readeth it. For the vision is yet for an appointed time, but at the end [he] shall speak…

The word “it” here is a personal pronoun that can be translated “he,” “she,” or “it.” In this case, it should be “he” because this is referring to the Holy Spirit. It continues:

…at the end [he] shall speak, and not lie: though [he] tarry, wait for [him]; because [he] will surely come, [he] will not tarry.

Do you see how God connects the watchman to a vision who will speak at the end?

This is very similar to Amos 3 where there is a watchman and then He speaks about revealing “his secret unto his servants the prophets.” Is it not interesting that Jesus said to “watch therefore”?

What is the church telling people to do? What are they telling people to do when they say, “No man knows the day or hour”? Are they not saying, “Relax! Relax! Get down from that rooftop! Get down from that tower! Here, sit down in your recliner and kick your feet up and relax!”

But what has happened to the watch? Where is the watching? If someone believes that there is no possibility for someone to know when Christ is coming, what would be the point of setting a watch? What would the watch see to warn the people? What would he tell them?

They tell them, “No man knows.” But what kind of a trumpet blast is this? When the church says that they live day-by-day expecting Christ to come because He could come at any day, “He could come today or He could come tomorrow,” do you really think that they are living to the glory of God? Do you really think that they are living in a way that enables them to learn, like we are learning, that Judgment Day is set and coming quickly?

Is the church really living in this kind of a way, or are they living more in a way that indicates that they believe that He is not coming at all? “He is not coming today and He is not coming tomorrow, even though I, of course, would profess that I would want Him to come and that I would love for Him to come. However, I really do not want Him to come.” Therefore, the church of our day lives just like the world lives, and the world has no understanding of these things. But Christ set the watch; and when the watchman sees the sword, he is to blow the trumpet.

Do you have a trumpet? Does anyone have a trumpet on them? If someone pulls one out, I am going to be shocked. Do you have a trumpet? I do not have a trumpet. I do not have one, not even in my car or at home. You would not want to hear me try to sound the trumpet either. I would guess that most of you also do not have a trumpet. Okay; then how are you going to sound the trumpet? How am I going to sound it? How are we going to blow the trumpet to warn the people? What is the trumpet?

Let us go to Revelation 1:10-11. This is God moving the Apostle John to write:

I was in the Spirit on the Lord’s day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet, Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a book…

This is referring to Jesus. He is definitely the “Alpha and Omega, the first and the last,” and His voice is like a trumpet. It is not a literal trumpet; it is “as of a trumpet.” It has great authority and great power; and so God likens His voice, in this case, to a trumpet.

Look at Revelation 4:1:

After this I looked, and, behold, a door was opened in heaven: and the first voice which I heard was as it were of a trumpet talking with me; which said, Come up hither, and I will show thee things which must be hereafter.

The trumpet has to do with speech. It has to do with talking. This is not just referring to anyone; this is referring to God’s speech, the Word of God. Christ is that voice of the trumpet and He is “the Word.” He is “the Word…made flesh.” Therefore, when we sound the trumpet, we are actually sharing what the Bible is saying. This is what this means.

Go to Isaiah 58. It says in Isaiah 58:1:

God is here giving command to His people to lift up their voices like a trumpet and to share the Gospel. The trumpet sounds when we personally speak to someone else about the Gospel, either one-on-one or in a group of people. We are sharing information from the Bible and this is a way of sounding the trumpet. When we hand people tracts, like “May 21, 2011 Judgment Day!” or “HOLY GOD will bring Judgment Day on MAY 21, 2011,” we are sounding the trumpet. When we wear a T-shirt that says something about Judgment Day being May 21, 2011, we are sounding the trumpet. When we put a bumper sticker on our car or when we put a magnetic sign on our car or when we wrap the vehicle with this information, we are sounding the trumpet.

Some sound this trumpet a little louder than others, but we are sounding the trumpet. We are declaring what God has said. We have heard from Him and now we are sharing this with others. This is what God is telling us, “Yes, do this. Lift up your voice like a trumpet and let it go. Blast away and warn the people. This is not the time to hold back.”

Like the watchman in the watchtower at midnight when the people are sleeping who says that he does not want to blow the trumpet because it would wake the people up, he must blow the trumpet. If they continue to sleep, they are going to die. The enemy army is almost there. He sees them. He knows that they are right at the gates. He, therefore, must blow the trumpet and warn the people so that they can take warning.

This is the big reason as to why God has opened up this information at this time. It is because He does want to warn everyone. He wants everyone to know what He is about to do.

Let us also go to Jeremiah 6. We read in Jeremiah 6:10:

Here God is saying that He is going to speak and give warning, but the problem is that the listeners cannot hear it. They cannot hear it because they have no ears to hear.

Look at verse 17, Jeremiah 6:17:

Again, this is not a literal trumpet. God is saying that He is giving words to warn the people, and yet they cannot hear it.

This is actually telling us that in their stubbornness and in their arrogance and in their pride, man is saying that not only are they not going to do what God says but they are going to tell God directly that they will not do it, “We will not hearken!” In other words, they are hearing the trumpet on one level, or else they would say, “I do not hear anything. I do not hear a trumpet. I do not hear this sound.”

I have no intention of doing this. I am going to live my life as I please”; and God is fully aware that this is how many people are treating this information at this time of the coming of the Lord Jesus Christ.

First of all, what is this telling us about what is coming? The watchman is seeing that the sword is coming, and a sword is what the enemy has in his hand when he is coming to kill, when he is coming to do war and to make battle.

So the idea that Christ could come today or maybe tomorrow or maybe next year or maybe one hundred years from now is extremely unclear. For something that would be this unclear, no one would prepare themselves for the battle.

It would be like hearing that a hurricane could come today. It is then that you would run around. If you are told that a hurricane might not come until next week, then you would relax. And if you are told that a hurricane might come next month or ten years from now or who knows when it could come, then you are not going to board up your house and you are not going to gather your most precious belongings and you certainly are not going to get in a traffic jam to flee the area.

You would not do any of these things because it is an unclear warning, but this is very clear and definite; and only God could have done this. God is the only One who has put the trumpets in the hands of His people to warn others that this is it.

I feel bad for the children; I do. It is kind of like imagining that we are back in history, maybe in the days of the kings of Israel when all of the enemies that Israel had were there. We can just imagine that an enemy is coming against the city. The watchman is in the tower and he sees the enemy coming, and so he blows the trumpet because this is his city and he cares about the people.

This is actually an act of love. They can call it crazy. They can call it whatever they want that people are wearing shirts and hats and putting bumper stickers on their cars and wrapping their vehicles with this warning. They can speak badly of this if they want to. They can even be ashamed of this if maybe someone from their own family is doing this and their friends are taking notice. However, they should know that this is a big act of love. This is why this is going on. This is why people cannot be silenced. This is why we have to do whatever we can do, whatever possible thing we can do to warn the people, and this is going to offend some.

But just imagine this village after the watchman sounds the trumpet. A mother who is so concerned for her child runs after her child who is in a group of other children playing and having fun. She scoops the child up and starts running because she heard the sound of the trumpet; and so the little boy says, “Mommy, I was having fun. I was playing. I want to go back.” But the mom says, “No, you do not understand. The watchman has sounded the trumpet. It is the alarm of war”; and so he just insists and cries and kicks his feet.

So was this a cruel thing that this mother did? Or did this mother deeply love her child and realize that the child does not understand. Maybe the child just did not understand that particular sound and what it means? It means the end, the end, and it is time to warn the people and to do whatever we can do.

For anyone who is getting tired of this, on one hand, I can understand. You might be getting tired of hearing this. Well, here is what God says in Revelation 18:21-22, as He speaks of Babylon:

And a mighty angel took up a stone like a great millstone, and cast it into the sea, saying, Thus with violence shall that great city Babylon be thrown down, and shall be found no more at all. And the voice of harpers, and musicians, and of pipers, and trumpeters, shall be heard no more at all in thee…

So you will not have to put up with this much longer. The trumpet will cease soon. It is going to be quieted because God will remove the Gospel from this world, as well as all of His people, and then there will be no more warning because it will be upon us. It will be here and there will be no mercy at that time for those who thought that there was “peace and safety.”

Let us stop here.
