Mankind, in a place called “hell,” would continue to rebel forever and ever and ever, and the amount of sins that they could heap up into eternity would dwarf anything that this world has ever seen. It makes no sense for God the King, who wants to put down this rebellion, to house criminals in order that they can continue their rebellious nature against Him and blaspheme Him forever.

Rather, He puts them down. He destroys them. He wipes them out at the end of this five-month period, and then that will be it. Then there will be no more rebellion. This is what an earthly king would do – he would just put them to death – and this is what God is going to do.

So the 200 million, which we are hopefully all a part of, are coming with fire, smoke, and brimstone.

Let us look at a passage in relation to this. Let us go to Psalm 11:5-6:

JEHOVAH trieth the righteous: but the wicked and him that loveth violence his soul hateth. Upon the wicked he shall rain snares, fire and brimstone, and an horrible tempest: this shall be the portion of their cup.

I do not want to get into this right now. Maybe during the next study, we will get into this. But this is speaking of the wicked and their cup. “Cup” in the Bible has to do with judgment, “the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath.” Spiritually speaking, Jesus drank of this cup in the garden. Remember that He prayed, “Let this cup pass from me.” There are many other verses that highlight this that we will take a look at next time. But this has to do with the cup of judgment and this comes to the wicked.

So, too, we recognize the language of “fire and brimstone, and an horrible tempest.” This fits in with everything that we were thinking. But why does it say, “He shall rain snares, fire and brimstone”? Why the word “snares”? Is this literal?

Actually, we were not even thinking that literal fire and brimstone would fall during the five months. We just realized that this was language that was expressing God’s wrath. We thought that the earth would be a huge mess, but I do not think that any of us were thinking that literal fire and brimstone would fall from the sky like it did upon Sodom. We were not thinking this at all.

So, here, God is helping us to get away from the idea of literal fire and brimstone, because it rained “snares, fire and brimstone.” “Snares” is also a word that we will look at a little later, but “snares” are just as it sounds. It is a trap; and it is not just one trap, it is plural. “Snares, fire and brimstone” will fall.

Let us close with Deuteronomy 29:20-23:

JEHOVAH will not spare him, but then the anger of JEHOVAH and his jealousy shall smoke against that man, and all the curses that are written in this book shall lie upon him…

This is speaking of the unsaved individual in the church or out of the church; it all applies.

It continues:

…and JEHOVAH shall blot out his name from under heaven. And JEHOVAH shall separate him unto evil out of all the tribes of Israel, according to all the curses of the covenant that are written in this book of the law: So that the generation to come of your children that shall rise up after you, and the stranger that shall come from a far land, shall say, when they see the plagues of that land, and the sicknesses which JEHOVAH hath laid upon it; And that the whole land thereof is brimstone, and salt, and burning, that it is not sown, nor beareth, nor any grass groweth therein, like the overthrow of Sodom, and Gomorrah, Admah, and Zeboim, which JEHOVAH overthrew in his anger, and in his wrath:

This is referring to individuals who are unsaved. They broke the law of God, and it does not matter which law. If someone breaks the law, they are a transgressor. In the book of James, we read, “For whosoever shall keep the whole law, and yet offend in one point, he is guilty of all.”

So they broke the law. They never had a Saviour, and so they are cursed. God then speaks of the curse that comes, the judgment of fire and brimstone. But notice that when a land is experiencing fire and brimstone, that land is not sown. That land is not sown. The sowing of seed is not done.

We find in the Gospel accounts the parable of the sower where God repeatedly makes reference to the fact that He is referring to His Word. But now, this sowing of the Gospel seed is not done.

As Abraham pleaded for Lot, but really for the righteous in Sodom, he would ask, “Wilt thou also destroy the righteous with the wicked?” The Lord would not destroy the righteous with the wicked, whether there be 50, 40, 30, 20, all the way down. And the Lord made a point concerning this of Lot in 2 Peter 2 when He said, “For that righteous man dwelling among them, in seeing and hearing, vexed his righteous soul from day to day with their unlawful deeds.”

So God tells us that Abraham was interceding for the righteous. Then He tells us that Lot was righteous. Then He also tells us that He told Lot, “I cannot do any thing till thou be come thither,” and then Lot enters into Zoar; because, as Abraham said, “That be far from thee to do after this manner, to slay the righteous with the wicked.”

Likewise, God held back judgment for centuries, for thousands of years. He did this because He had a plan to save His people, His elect, His righteous ones, those whom He would make righteous.

He could not rain down fire and brimstone on the world, spiritually, until the last of His elect, like Lot, made it to the spiritual Zoar, which is Christ. Once the last of God’s people became saved, then it began. It was then that it began.

There is fire and brimstone from Heaven falling now. This explains why Family Radio, the most evangelistic – and this just means the sending forth of the Gospel – the most evangelistic organization that the world has ever seen, has now stopped. It has stopped sending out the Gospel. It is not sending out tract teams to other countries. The caravans are not running. It is not mailing out tracts to individuals. And its teaching is no longer warning people that they only have a month or ten days or a day left.

We are not telling people this about October 21. I do not think that we should have a countdown clock. We are not sending the Gospel to the world in order that they can respond and repent, by God’s grace and by His doing, because this is no longer possible.

This is as we read in Revelation 22:10-12:

And he saith unto me, Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand. He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is filthy, let him be filthy still: and he that is righteous, let him be righteous still: and he that is holy, let him be holy still. And, behold, I come quickly; and my reward is with me…

So this is where we stand. Whatever you are in God’s sight, you are either spiritually righteous or filthy. We can have someone who is a Mr. Clean, and yet he is spiritually filthy in God’s sight due to his sins.

Whatever God did or did not do in your life prior to May 21, there is now where you are. If He saved you, you are righteous and holy. If He did not save you, you are unclean, you are filthy in His sight, and everyone in this condition will remain this way. There is now no possibility of change from one spiritual condition to another, as far as what is going on in someone’s heart, for these five months.

But, again, we do not know. We do not know who is saved or who is not saved. God could have made a change in someone, and yet they do not even know this. This is why we keep praying for our friends and our family and our neighbors. We need to continue to do this even for ourselves. If we are unsure of our salvation, it could still be that God did make a change and that this is just not evident in your life as yet.
