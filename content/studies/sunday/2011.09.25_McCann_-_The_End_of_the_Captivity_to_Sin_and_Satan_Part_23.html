Let us go to Revelation 15. In Revelation 15, we have seen over the last couple of studies that the seven messengers are the true believers. This verse alone proves this. We read in Revelation 15:6:

And the seven angels [messengers] came out of the temple, having the seven plagues, clothed in pure and white linen…

Revelation 19:8 tells us that pure and white linen is the “righteousness of saints.” This has nothing to do with angels, with angelic beings or spirit beings. They do not need the righteousness of Christ, but every one of the elect absolutely needs the covering of the pure and white linen. This is the covering over our sin. It is that spotless robe of Christ’s righteousness. This is what these seven messengers are clothed with, and so they cannot be angels.

Actually, these are the same ones that verse 2 is speaking about. Revelation 15:2 says:

And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God.

They are one and the same. They are those who got the victory over the beast.

Who are those who got the victory over the beast? They are God’s people who came out of great tribulation. These are the great multitude whom God saved, who came out of great tribulation. They were not in the churches where the “man of sin” had taken his seat and where he was ruling.

On top of this, God ransacked Satan’s kingdom. He did this at the cross when He bound him, but He did not actually redeem all of those whom He had saved from before the foundation of the world until just prior to May 21. Then everyone whose name was written in the Lamb’s Book of life was saved.

This was a great victory for the Gospel and for the Lord Jesus Christ. This is the victory that is in view and it is a victory over four things:

…victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name…

The “number of his name” would be 666, which we read about in Revelation 13.

Look at Revelation 17 where it mentions the beast. We read in Revelation 17:8:

The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit…

“The beast” is just another name for Satan, but this is a name that God primarily uses to identify his rule during the 23-year great tribulation. It continues:

…and go into perdition: and they that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is.

So this is indicating that Satan ruled prior to the cross. Christ bound him at the cross and that was like a deathblow. But then he was loosed on May 21, 1988, and so he “yet is.” This is what these three statements are referring to.

Then we read in Revelation 17:9-10:

This is referring to Satan’s time of ruling prior to the cross. It continues:

This is referring to the little season of great tribulation.

So the beast is “of the seven.” It is significant that God said in Revelation 17:9:

…The seven heads are seven mountains, on which the woman sitteth.

So the beast is likened to seven mountains.

Now think about these two things. The seven messengers, who are the true believers, got victory over the beast and the beast is likened to mountains.

This word “victory” from Revelation 15:2 is a word that is often translated as “overcome” and also “prevailed.” For example, we read In Revelation 5:5:

And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book…

He got the victory. That is the same word. This means to be victorious or to win.

What happened on May 21 was that God overcame the kingdom of Satan. He was victorious. The battle of Armageddon began. Immediately, God slew a third part of men and He is presently pouring out torment on the rest of mankind. Actually, on all of mankind; but those not associated with the churches are being tormented in the sense that they are not those who have already been killed. This is a great victory for God.

This is exactly the same thing that the Word of God does. God’s Word is a two-edged sword. On the one hand, it cuts to salvation. On the other hand, it cuts to judgment.

This helped me. I was looking through a concordance. Every place where I read “month,” I replace it with “new moon,” as in the fifteenth day of the new moon or in the twentieth day of the new moon.

This really helps us to see their mindset. They were looking in the sky. They did not have calendars like we do, so they would look in the sky for the first sign of the new moon.

What was the sign of the new moon? Well, there is a little bit of a question about this in some circles. But, really, a new moon, traditionally for the Jews and how God looks at this in the Bible, is the first sighting of the crescent moon or that sliver of moon that we can begin to see.

So the new moon begins in this way and then it gets fuller and fuller and fuller until it goes dark and black. Then that period is over and we wait for the next new moon.

Keep in mind, not literally but spiritually, what is the condition of our moon? It is black. Right now, it is dark. The next time the moon appears will be the new moon, which will be October 21.
