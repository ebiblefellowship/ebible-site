Chris: To begin with, people are saying this again. People are saying that we cannot know the day or hour, which is not new. There is no new revelation being made as far as that statement goes.

Have we heard this before? How often have we heard this? How much have we heard this? Are we to think that, suddenly, now at this time, after the five months have passed, this is a new revelation and that no man now can know the day or hour?

No; God is not doing this at all. This is just coming from those who are possibly kind of weary and those who may be tired because they believed that the Bible taught that they could know the time. Things did not work out the way that they thought; and so, now, they have had enough and they want to just join forces with everyone else, “Hey; I am right with you. The Bible says that no man knows.”

Well, the Bible does say, “Of that day and hour knoweth no man.” But we have to look at this in the light of the whole Bible. When we do, we find that the other information is still there. We find that God, before He destroyed the world with a flood, told Noah about it beforehand. We find that God, before He destroyed Sodom, told Lot all about it beforehand so that he could get out of the city. We find that God, before He said that He would destroy Nineveh, told Jonah to warn the people.

We have all of the other verses like, “Surely the Lord GOD will do nothing, but he revealeth his secret unto his servants the prophets” and “A wise man's heart discerneth both time and judgment.” We could go on with many more; but the response is, “Yes; but it did not happen.”

By God’s grace, I had the opportunity of being interviewed many, many times on the radio leading up to May 21, 2011. This was a common statement that the interviewer on the radio station would make. Callers would sometimes have the opportunity to call and ask me questions and they would say the same thing, again and again, without fail. “Look; there have been a lot of predictions,” they would say, “and none of them ever happened in the past.” They would just pick some number like “hundreds of predictions,” when nobody really knows; and so they would insist, “None of these predictions ever came to pass.”

Many of these stations were secular, and so it occurred to me, “Let me use a secular analogy that they can understand. Let me use an earthly illustration that they will be able to get.” So I used the illustration of flight and air travel.

How often through the history of man did people try to fly and fail? Therefore, as a result, how often did people say, “You cannot do it. You will never be able to fly”? They would say, “In fact, it is absolutely not possible to fly.” And all of history had proven that statement true up until the point that they made it.

Every time there would be an additional attempt and failure, this reinforced this idea, “We know that you cannot fly because this is an impossibility. If you try, you are going to end up at the bottom of the gully. Your wings are going to be broken.”

Yet what if people believed this? What if they finally said, “You know what? It is true. Hundreds of years have passed and the attempts have gotten more and more intense. After all, look at that last one! That guy ended up at the bottom of the rocks. You know what? These people are right. It is an impossibility to fly.”

What if they had believed this? Well, it would be a little harder traveling this Christmas season, would it not? It would be very hard to travel at this time.

God is in control of these things, too, and He put this in the hearts and minds of certain individuals, “I do not care what people think. I think that it is impossible.” They worked hard. God, of course, was in control of all of the technological increases that we have been blessed with and He opened up their understanding to tweak things a little here and to tweak things a little there. The next thing you know, they were taking flight.

It was a little hop and a skip at first. Then they could fly some distance. Now, of course, we can fly to the moon. We can fly to Mars with satellites. We can go pretty far from just 150 years ago or maybe a little bit longer when men would say, “It is an absolute impossibility to get up there into the sky.”

The point I was trying to make was that we recognize it on an earthly level. This is true for many things. There is always trial and error. A success or finding the solution or finding the cure to a disease is full of trial and error. It is only when someone is willing to continue that they finally come to the cure for this disease or that disease or there is an invention made that was seemingly not possible before.

Nothing in the Bible has changed and it is a set date that will not move. It has never moved; it is unmovable. The idea that God changes time regarding the end of the world is ridiculous. He knows the end from the beginning and He picked a certain date.

We were incorrect in thinking that this date was October 21, 2011, as far as the very end of time. We were correct that May 21, 2011 was Judgment Day. We were correct that there was a very important five-month period that did end on October 21, and we are not outside of the Bible’s calendar at this time.

I am not going to present this today; but within the next three to four weeks, we will find that the Bible locks in May 21, 2011 as the day of judgment and that it gives us a date for Judgment Day, the end of days.

This is coming from the Bible. This is from God’s calendar, and this is how He can say, “Though I tarry,” because He is the One who laid things all out so that we would come to the conclusion that it would be October 21, “I will not tarry”; because that set date that has always been there is still there.

1st Question: I started reading again in Genesis 7:11. I kept reading and got up to the eighth chapter. Lo and behold, as I began to read Genesis 8:13, God very clearly said that Noah was 601 years old, not 600. So he was in the ark for over a year and that was the first day of the first month. Then, lo and behold, I read the next verse and it said that the earth was dried up on the 2nd month, the 27th day. This shifts to a day in 2012, if we follow the same logic and convert our calendar back to the Biblical calendar. This comes out to be the 19th day of the 5th month on our calendar of May 2012. This is just the straight math of saying what God says, which was that Noah was in the ark for a year. He was trying to establish that Noah was another year older and he still had not come out of that ark, but he did on the 27th day of the 2nd month of the Biblical calendar. When you go to a computer and convert it, it gives the date of May the 19th. Have you studied this?

Chris: I appreciate those verses and I will try to address this. Those verses have been presented through emails. Let me say that I do not think that that is the day.

When we read that they came out of the ark on the 27th day of the 2nd month, that actually is about one year later, even though it would apparently be ten days beyond. This is because we have to keep in mind that the Hebrew calendar was lunar. This means that they would have one month of 29 days and the next month of 30 days. If you follow this for twelve months, you will end up with only 354 days.

So even though this is ten days beyond the 17th day when Noah went into the ark, since it says that he came out on the 27th, when you relate this to the year that God has established, which is 365.2422 days, then it comes out to be almost exactly one year that he was in the ark. Mike Cook did a study on this a couple of years ago.

In looking at the book of Esther, and I will try to layout some things today during the study, I see a very clear timeline and date. You are right, though. This is coming from the Bible and not from any other source; and so this had to be looked at as a possibility, but I do not think that this is it.

In regards to this since October 21 (just like after May 21), people have thought about three and a half days or forty days or Christmas or December 27th; but no. Anything that is trying to build onto October 21 or add onto October 21, we can be sure that it will be wrong. This is not what God is doing at all. We will get into the reasons for why I think this later.

 

2nd Question: Please elaborate on Hebrews 12:15-17.

Chris: We read in Hebrews 12:14-17:

Follow peace with all men, and holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord: Looking diligently lest any man fail of the grace of God; lest any root of bitterness springing up trouble you, and thereby many be defiled; Lest there be any fornicator, or profane person, as Esau, who for one morsel of meat sold his birthright. For ye know how that afterward, when he would have inherited the blessing, he was rejected: for he found no place of repentance, though he sought it carefully with tears.

Verse 15 has a good admonition for all of us regarding the possibility of a “root of bitterness springing up” and troubling us and defiling us.

First of all, this is an admonition to husbands. Do you remember what God said, “Husbands, love your wives, and be not bitter against them.” Why? This is because, sometimes, a wife might nag. Normally, the husband takes it and takes it and gets quiet, but he struggles inside and there can be bitterness that develops.

This is why God warns the husband to watch out for this and to not allow this. If your wife does something that is wrong towards you, forgive. This is where love comes in, “Husbands, love your wives, and be not bitter against them.”

This is what bitterness is a lot of times. It is internalizing something and dwelling on it. It is kind of meditating on an evil thing, but the Bible tells us to meditate on the Word of God. That is where our minds should be. But with bitterness, we mediate on somebody who hurt us or something that we feel was done unjustly to us. We dwell on that and we start getting angry and bitter inside.

I think some people have done this maybe in regards to the money and the time that they spent in getting out the Gospel leading up to May 21. Since this did not happen as planned, they are getting bitter. You can hear them say things like, “I spent thousands of dollars going on trips.”

First of all, nobody made anyone do any of these things. That was all their own doing. They also say things like, “I did not focus on other things. I did not buy this other thing that I wanted. I took funds that I could have used for some other things and used that to get the Gospel out.”

Now that it is after that day and things did not turn out, as they look back, they could be getting angry, which leads to bitterness. But we have to remember that if we had an opportunity to take up our cross and follow Christ and use our time and our money and our resources in order to get the Gospel out to people to warn them, this was exactly what we should have been doing all along anyway.

Did we do anything special? Does God not say that we are to sacrifice our whole lives and that we are to give Him everything that we have? What does it say in Romans 12? We read in Romans 12:1:

I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that ye present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is…

Then does this say that this is a tremendously great and wonderful thing that we are doing? No; this does not say that. It says:

…which is your reasonable service.

This is our “reasonable service.” It is our duty and this applies throughout all of history. This applies to any time, to any reader who would read this throughout the centuries. Our reasonable duty is to give up our lives and to sacrifice ourselves.

Some people, you see, were prompted to do something that they would not normally have done, but they thought that Christ was coming. Some were true believers and had no problem with these things. They look back and are thankful that they had the opportunity to see what it was like to live in a wholehearted way for Christ. But some who look back are now sorry that they gave up their time and their money, and so forth, and they can be getting bitter about this.

So God is warning us to be careful and to watch out for this, and this has to do with the type of ground that the seed falls on in the parable of the sowing of the seed. We read about the parable of the sower in Matthew 13.

 

3rd Question: In Esther 1 and Genesis 23, we find the number 127; but in the Hebrew, these are written as three separate numbers. Should we look at them as 100, 20, and 7 individually for spiritual meaning in these chapters?

Chris: No; we should not do this. This is just how God wrote this. This is how He writes out numbers. Sometimes we read something like “threescore and ten” where things seem to be broken down into different numbers. But when God speaks of 127, we get that number of 127.

What we can do, when possible, is to then break down that number by dividing it to see if it divides by 2 or 3 or 4, or by whatever number. But 127 does not divide; it stands alone and cannot be broken down into any smaller numbers.

As far as the number 127, we just look at how it is used in the book of Esther or wherever it is found. In this case, we find it also in Genesis in association with Sarah and we see that it has to do with all of the elect, because Sarah is “the mother of us all,” as it says in Galatians, or all of the true believers. And we find this number in relation to “all the provinces,” which were 127.

So, basically, when we find, again and again, in the book of Esther that the decree of the king is being sent to all 127 provinces, this means all of the world. When the Jews in all 127 provinces, for instance, hear that there is a set day of judgment for them in which they are to be killed and all of the Jews begin to mourn in all of the provinces, that is telling us that there is some sort of announcement that has been made to the world in which all of the elect hear it and respond.

So we are helped with this identification with Sarah and with the number of provinces. They are tying together the elect who are found in all of the world, as Ahasuerus is a picture of God who reigns over the Kingdom of God; and so all of these provinces typify His kingdom, especially in this world.

5th Question: I remember Brother Camping saying that God has many names. He assigned Himself many names, like “lily of the valleys” or “bright and morning star” or “Jesus.” From my understanding, the name “Jesus” is the timeline name that God incarnated. When He assigned to Abraham another name, from Abram to Abraham, would you say that He changed his name or that He assigned him another name? So did God change His name from “Jehovah” to “Jesus,” or did He just assign Himself another name? “Jehovah” is not mentioned in the New Testament. What is mentioned is “the Father.” Can we say that “the Father” is “Jehovah the Spirit” and that “Jesus” is just the timeline name when He was incarnated, but that they are both the same?

Chris: I would just say that He has many names and that each name teaches us something about God Himself. For instance, it says in Isaiah that they shall “call his name Immanuel.” And it says in Matthew 1:21:

And she shall bring forth a son, and thou shalt call his name JESUS: for he shall save his people from their sins.

Then we also read in Matthew 1:23:

Behold, a virgin shall be with child, and shall bring forth a son, and they shall call his name Emmanuel, which being interpreted is, God with us.

So He is “Emmanuel.” He is also “Jesus.” He is also “Jehovah,” because Isaiah 43 tells us, “I, even I, am JEHOVAH; and beside me there is no saviour.” I would not say that He changed His name, but that God has given Himself many names.

 

6th Question: Please read Luke 19:11-15. I believe that verses 11, 12, and 15 are teaching one and the same. But because verse 11 says that Jesus was “nigh to Jerusalem” and because the disciples thought that the Kingdom of God “should immediately appear,” can we relate this to post May 21? If so, can we then relate the specific events and instructions in the “occupy till I come” parable to how we should be behaving at this time?

Chris: We read in Luke 19:11-15:

And as they heard these things, he added and spake a parable, because he was nigh to Jerusalem, and because they thought that the kingdom of God should immediately appear. He said therefore, A certain nobleman went into a far country to receive for himself a kingdom, and to return. And he called his ten servants, and delivered them ten pounds, and said unto them, Occupy till I come. But his citizens hated him, and sent a message after him, saying, We will not have this man to reign over us. And it came to pass, that when he was returned, having received the kingdom, then he commanded these servants to be called unto him, to whom he had given the money, that he might know how much every man had gained by trading.

I have not looked at verse 11 that closely, so I cannot really comment on which time period it is really focused on. I do know that the verse that says “occupy till I come” applies to us. Of course, the question is how do we occupy?

We occupy by doing the will of God, whatever the will of God is. This is how believers occupy.

We are thinking, and Lord willing this might happen, of developing some information that is primarily from the book of Esther and putting together a booklet or a tract and putting this on our website. This will be done in humility. We may say something like “Will Christ return” on such-and-such a date “during the Feast of Purim?” I do not know exactly how this will be worded. We will have this in big bold letters on the website with a link to the studies. This information will go out and people will be able to read it, and this is a form of occupying.

Let us say that God was still saving, which I do not really believe that He is, but let us say that He was. Did the fact that believers did not tell people this for five months prevent God from saving anyone during that five months? Would it or could it? No. How does God save? He saves through His Word.

Some people have always been under the mistaken idea that you have to preach Christ and Him crucified. By that, they mean that you have to keep talking about the cross. But Christ is the Word. He is the whole Bible; and what we have to do is to just teach truth. This is all that we have ever had to do.

We do not have to specifically talk about any aspect of salvation in order for God to save if He were saving. God can save with a word. He can save with a genealogy. He does not specifically need people to say that He is saving.

But I think that we are going to occupy in this manner in the coming days and the reason is coming from the Bible. This is because in the book of Esther, there is a spiritual chronology. It starts with Vashti who represents Israel. It moves on to the virgins who were gathered in, which typify the New Testament church. Right in order, it goes to the two doorkeepers who were killed, which represented the end of the church age. Then it goes in order to the advancement of Haman, which typifies Satan’s loosing at the time of the great tribulation. It goes into May 21 and dwells there for over three chapters in the book of Esther. Then it goes into post-May 21 during the last couple of chapters.

So the book of Esther gives us this chronology. Within this chronology, the Word is sent out again. This is because once Haman is killed, all of the Jews in all of the provinces are notified. All of the posts are sent out again. The only setting for that sending out the Word again is after May 21. And so for this reason, which is a Biblical reason that is coming from God, we will want to notify people about this information.

The world heard about May 21. In a certain sense, they heard about October 21. No one has heard about Purim at all.
