1st Question: Being that Christmas is soon, I was wondering if the Bible says anything about Christmas being so commercialized and that they do not have the true meaning of Christ’s birth and who Christ is. People celebrate this in a commercial sense with Santa Claus.

Chris: Is there any day of the year when people do what is right? Is there any day of the year when people do what they should do? It is not only Christmas. Yes, it is true that this is a day that we have set aside to observe the birth of Christ and really to thank God and to give glory to Him that He did, through Jesus, enter into the world. But every day, really, should be a day when we wake up and say, “Today is a day that the Lord has made and let us rejoice and be glad in it. Let us serve Him. Let us do His will.”

Does mankind ever do this? Certain ones, only by God’s grace, are the ones who realize, “Okay; today is God’s day and I want to do His will today.”

This is the case with Christmas. We cannot expect people who are far from God and in darkness and who have no real understanding of the Bible or of Christ to properly observe that day.

So we can just be thankful if we hear a hymn or if we hear a carol that is mentioning some truth from the Bible. We can be thankful for opportunities where maybe we can share something. But there is no sense in being upset because the world is using that day as a worldly day, like they do every other day. This is always going to be the case because we are never going to convince the world to live as they should because they are part of the world.

 

2nd Question: Could you look at Matthew 2 where Herod was tricked by the magi? They had to go another way. The angel of the Lord told them to go another way and to flee into Egypt because Herod was going to seek the child to destroy him. When Herod found out that he was tricked, he had all the babies killed from two years old and under. Did this have something to do with Herod’s jealousy or that he thought that the kingdom was going to be taken away from him? What are your thoughts on this?

Chris: In Matthew 2:11-12, this is speaking of the wise men who came from the east. It says:

And when they were come into the house…

They did not come into a stable; and so the Christmas cards that you see with them going into the stable are incorrect. They went into the house and then it says:

…they saw the young child with Mary his mother…

They did not see a baby, because He was older at this point. It continues:

…and fell down, and worshipped him: and when they had opened their treasures, they presented unto him gifts; gold, and frankincense, and myrrh. And being warned of God in a dream that they should not return to Herod, they departed into their own country another way.

Herod realizes that he was deceived. You are right. He was very jealous. He loved power and the authority that he had. He was a proud man.

It is amazing the depths that people can sink to in lust for some sin, whatever the sin happens to be. When sin takes hold of a person, we can really see the depths of depravity. There is not total depravity because God has always instilled a conscience element into man to where man can still feel guilty to a degree. But there certainly are great depths of depravity that people can sink down into.

Mankind can go pretty far into the mire and into the mud, and Herod went that way out of lust for power and authority. He did not want anyone to dare take his kingdom, even though he was an old man and was actually going to die. Historical sources tell us that he did die in 4 B.C. Jesus was born in 7 B.C. By the time that the wise men got to Jesus, He was already a toddler.

So that was very close to the point when Herod would actually physically die, and he was still doing evil things to seek to remain in power and in control. But what good did this do him? What good did this do him at all? This did nothing for him. And what misery did he cause others when he slew all the children from two years and under in that certain area? Historically, he was an evil man; and history is full of them.

But in the book of Revelation, God tells us what this was all a picture of. We read in Revelation 12:2-4 about a woman:

And she being with child cried, travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered. And there appeared another wonder in heaven; and behold a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads. And his tail drew the third part of the stars of heaven, and did cast them to the earth: and the dragon stood before the woman which was ready to be delivered, for to devour her child as soon as it was born.

This is what Herod pictured. He pictured Satan who wanted to kill Christ.

So Satan stirred up this wicked king, Herod, to kill the baby. If he could do this, he would accomplish his master’s wish, which was to destroy the baby, the Lord Jesus Christ; and yet, underneath it all, it was actually Satan working to kill the baby.

Then it says in Revelation 12:5:

And she brought forth a man child, who was to rule all nations with a rod of iron: and her child was caught up unto God, and to his throne.

So we know that it is definitely Christ who is in view here in Revelation 12. He was brought forth from a woman, which is how God pictures the believers. As Jesus came down the line in Israel, He was delivered into history and then Satan wanted to kill him and to destroy him but God, of course, spared Jesus and protected Him; and then later, Jesus was caught up. He was resurrected and then He went up forty days after.

 

3rd Question: Since this is the Christmas holiday season, we have been singing Christmas songs, but there is this one song that bothers me. It refers to Christ as “the son of Mary.” I know that Jesus acknowledged Himself as the Son of God and as the Son of man, but it seems that He did not acknowledge Himself as the son of Mary. As a matter of fact, He called her “woman.” Can you say something about this?

Chris: The problem is that people say that Mary is the mother of God. That is a wrong statement. That is not true, because God is eternal. Certainly, she did not begat God. But in the sense that God entered into the human race in a human form and in a physical body, Mary was His mother. She did give birth. In that sense only, we can say that Mary was the mother of Jesus.

Additional Comments: I have a couple of women friends. I argue with them and they argue with me. One is Catholic. The other was Catholic but became a Biblical Christian and a Family Radio listener. We were arguing about how God technically put Jesus in Mary’s womb. I said that it was possible that she could have been a surrogate. Maybe, literally, God used that seed in her, but God did not tell us technically how He did this.

Chris: Well, He was conceived by the Holy Spirit. The Bible tells us this. How did He make Adam and Eve? He just created them. Since then, there is a natural process between men and women, but that is why it was a miraculous birth. He was born of a virgin.

It needed to be this way because He could not have had an earthly father. If Joseph were Jesus’ father, then He would not have been sinless because you cannot bring a clean from an unclean. We read this in the book of Job, “Who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean? not one.”

Christ was eternal God and was without sin. In order for Him to be born without the taint of sin, He had to be born of God and without an earthly Father. But He also had to be born of a woman, but in this case a virgin; and so God made it very clear that His birth had nothing to do with the natural way in which children enter into the world. This was a miraculous birth.

Additional Comments: How about the biological make up? Could God have just put an embryo there or a body there?

Chris: I do not think that we should get into that area. How ever He did it, we know that He was conceived. He was an embryo and went through all of the stages. All of the stages that ever other child goes through, Christ went through. We just need to leave it there.

Additional Comments: I wondered if God used the biological part of Mary. But then, it is not important. Right?

Chris: We know that He was born with a body.

Additional Comments: Someone from Paltalk offers Matthew 1:21:

And she shall bring forth a son, and thou shalt call his name JESUS: for he shall save his people from their sins.

 

4th Question: Would you please comment on the message from Mr. Camping where he mentioned that Jesus is the light of the world and that since nothing shows that this light has gone out, this emphasizes that salvation has to still be possible? With this being mentioned, would this not mean that May 21, 2011 was not Judgment Day?

Chris: It is not as simple as that. We have this statement from Mr. Camping that is written and on the Family Radio website. This is a relatively short statement. In it, he mentions a couple of verses that bring up all sorts of questions.

Until these questions are answered, personally, I could never hand out a tract right now. I would be convicted by my conscience at this point.

Mr. Camping went to John 9:5. It says in John 9:4-5:

I must work the works of him that sent me, while it is day: the night cometh, when no man can work. As long as I am in the world, I am the light of the world.

Verse 4 indicates that there is a period of night. When does this night come? We thought that we understood this.

We also have to factor in Matthew 24:29:

Immediately after the tribulation of those days shall the sun be darkened, and the moon shall not give her light, and the stars shall fall from heaven, and the powers of the heavens shall be shaken:

Now we have a question to answer. Certain circumstances will not allow, as this is God’s will, for Mr. Camping to be on the “Open Forum” program to be asked these things at this time. But was May 21, 1988 to May 21, 2011 the great tribulation? Did it end on May 21, 2011? If so, immediately after, the sun was darkened and the moon did not give its light.

How do you explain this? This is not a simple thing. This is not something that can be quickly stated where, all of a sudden, we have an answer. There are a lot of other questions that come up that have to be answered.

What was that 23 years? What was May 21, 2011? Was it Judgment Day or was it not Judgment Day?

Personally, I feel that May 21, 2011 was the last day of the great tribulation and that it was Judgment Day. Therefore, verses like Matthew 24:29 spiritually come into play in that the sun is darkened and the moon is not giving its light, and so on. Also on May 21, 2011, we have the additional confirmation that it was 7,000 years from the flood, the day that the door was shut.

I understand that we are in a period of time now where there are a lot of questions and a lot of uneasiness, and yet I think that one problem is that Mr. Camping is not aware of some information that is in the book of Esther that I hope we can look at in the next few weeks. This information will reaffirm some things and also will give us a date for the end, but I just do not want to lay this out right now.

 

5th Question: I have two different questions. One is about Psalm 58:10. There was some teaching in the last couple of months that there may be a simultaneous resurrection or rapture of the true believers with the nonbelievers passing on. Through some research, it was found that the unsaved may pass on in a blink of an eye. In relation to Psalm 58:10, could you explain the fact that we might see God’s wrath on the unbelievers?

Chris: You are referring to the actual day when Christ comes. Let us read Psalm 58:10:

The righteous shall rejoice when he seeth the vengeance: he shall wash his feet in the blood of the wicked.

This is referring to Christ.

Additional Comments: The last part looks like Christ.

Chris: The first part does, too. He is “the righteous.” Romans says, “There is none righteous, no, not one,” and so this is not referring to any of us. We have imputed righteousness, but He is The Righteous One. This is why it is singular: “when he seeth the vengeance.” It does not say: “when they see the vengeance.”

They are going to realize that what is going on is about God. It has nothing to do with the Mayans, especially since their date is several months off. Wrong is wrong, whether it is one day or several months, so I do not think that it enters into it.

Additional Comments: There has been so much in the media trying to put down 2011, which was coming from the Bible, and trying to ignore God and His plan concerning the coming of the end.

Chris: You can almost tell when something is false by the amount of media coverage it is prone to get and the playfulness with which it is presented.

In the case of May 21, the world was forced to finally discuss the end in a very uncomfortable way. But with the Mayan calendar, whenever you hear reports, it is a joke.

Additional Comments: If He does not come in 2011, it will look like we were wrong in the studies that we have from the Bible, when other people at this time, because nothing happened on May 21 like we said, have switched over in their thinking.

Chris: We were wrong as far as the understanding of what May 21 was and what October 21 was. I would say that God not only allowed it but wanted it to be this way.

This is how we understand the verse in Habakkuk, “Though [he] tarry…[he] will not tarry.” This is because God allowed this understanding of a certain date to appear as though He tarried; but in truth, He has never tarried for a moment. He has always had a set date that will be the end.

We have to remember that the first earth went 6,023 years. That is where we get the 23 from now, as we are in the year 13,023. We get it from the first earth and the tribulation period that was adjoined with that. But from the flood, we are in the year 7,000. We are not in 7,023. We are in the year 7,000 all the way into the year 2012; but how God totals out the first earth and the second earth is His business.

Additional Comments: If there is a space, like we read in Revelation 11, where we would go up and these people would be suffering at that point, God would be saying that He shares His glory with only a certain group of people.

Chris: When Christ comes, there will not be another five months or anything like that.

Additional Comments: I am just wondering if there is a difference between when we are raptured and for those left behind.

And it came to pass, that when the gates of Jerusalem began to be dark before the sabbath, I commanded that the gates should be shut, and charged that they should not be opened till after the sabbath: and some of my servants set I at the gates, that there should no burden be brought in on the sabbath day.

9th Question: Could you look at Revelation 10:1-8 pertaining to there no longer being time? Do you know anything about that?

Chris: Let us look at Revelation 10:6-7:

And sware by him that liveth for ever and ever, who created heaven, and the things that therein are, and the earth, and the things that therein are, and the sea, and the things which are therein, that there should be time no longer: But in the days of the voice of the seventh angel, when he shall begin to sound, the mystery of God should be finished…

By the way, the word “begin” is the word “about,” which is found in Revelation 10:4 where it says “I was about to write.”

So when John saw the vision, he had pen in hand and he was about to write, but he did not write. This is the same word here where it says, “when he shall begin to sound.” So this was when he was “about to sound,” but he had not sounded. He was about to sound that “the mystery of God should be finished.”

Revelation 10 continues to be a difficult chapter. I think that it is going to take more study. I am not ready to say anything more about it.

 

10th Question: October 21 was the Feast of Ingathering and Tabernacles. With the teaching of Esther, are we at the point in time when God is gathering in His elect and we are dwelling in booths?

Chris: It is interesting that the Feast of Ingathering does identify with the latter rain, which was 17 years; then it came to an end. The rain stopped falling, the door shut on May 21, and we had a period of 153 days. We read about 153 fish in John 21 and all of these fish were caught in the net and were dragged to shore to Jesus, to where Jesus was.

So it appears that all of the elect were saved by May 21. Then the process of the 153 days began wherein all of the elect were being brought unto the last day of the Feast of Tabernacles in order to be presented in some sort of way before the Lord, because God emphasizes repeatedly that we are not to appear before Him empty, that the fruits were to be gathered in before we kept the feast.

Obviously, the last day of the Feast of Tabernacles was not the last day of the end of the world; so we misunderstood this. We were incorrect as to the significance of the Feast of Tabernacles.

That is the benefit of the book of Esther because it does not deal with the Feast of Tabernacles. It gives a direct time path from one point to another.

We are still trying to understand other information. Why the five months? There is a five-month period laid out in the Bible. Zechariah 1 helps to confirm this, and so that was not incorrect. God, for His own purposes, wanted to stress May 21 as Judgment Day and He also wanted an emphasis on October 21.

Also, this all helps to lay out the period of the “fiery trial” in order for His people to be patient, as we see in James 1. To define “patience,” let us read this. I never really noticed something before seeing this recently. We read in James 1:3:

Knowing this, that the trying of your faith worketh patience.

You must have patience “after ye have done the will of God” to “receive the promise.” You are in “need of patience.” What works patience? It is the “trial of your faith.”

Are we in a period of trial of faith? Certainly! Have we “done the will of God”? Normally, we would question, “What? What kind of question is this? Every single day, we have the obligation to do the will of God.”

This is true in many ways; but we have to ask, “What does God mean when He said ‘after ye have done the will of God’”? It cannot be that after we have done the will of God, we go right to Heaven. If this were true, we would have no “need of patience.”

So there has to be a period of time after you have done the will of God until you go to Heaven wherein you have need of patience, which is a period defined as the trying of faith. This is where we are.

Of course, when God, for His own purposes (and this is one of them), proclaims a date and moves His people to share this with the world and that date passes and it appears He has tarried, instantly, we can see, especially at this period of time, the trying of all of His people’s faith.

 

11th Question: In Nehemiah 9:3, it speaks of “the LORD their God” and the “fourth part,” and then it repeats this. Of course, this is talking about the Hebrews. Why does it say “the LORD their God” and not just “the Lord God.” Would that not be more appropriate? Could you help me with this?

 

Chris: Let us read Nehemiah 9:3:

And they stood up in their place, and read in the book of the law of JEHOVAH their God one fourth part of the day; and another fourth part they confessed, and worshipped JEHOVAH their God.

So your question is why it says “their God” and not “the God.”

Well, the answer is that God is the God of all men. He created all men, but He has a special relationship with His people who are typified by the Jews here. The Jews were the people of God, the “chosen ones” out of all of the earth, even though many of them were not saved. God was their God in a way that He was not the God of all of the rest of the people of the world.

It is the same thing today. God is the God of the child of God in a special way, because He does not have this relationship with just anybody. This is why it says “Jehovah their God.”
