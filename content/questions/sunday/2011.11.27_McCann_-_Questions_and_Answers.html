1st Question: I think that it is in the book of Leviticus where it clearly states that if a man divorces his wife, he cannot get remarried for as long as his former wife is living.

Chris: God had a law in the Old Testament that allowed Him to divorce Israel. It is in Deuteronomy 24:1:

When a man hath taken a wife, and married her, and it come to pass that she find no favour in his eyes, because he hath found some uncleanness in her: then let him write her a bill of divorcement, and give it in her hand, and send her out of his house.

So God had this law on the books in order that He could put away Israel, but then when Jesus was asked by the Pharisees concerning Moses having said that you could give a writing of divorcement, then He rescinded the law and said, “But from the beginning it was not so” and “What therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder.”

From that point, never again was there to be divorce. Israel was a corporate entity. Many of them were not even saved. God married them for His own purposes. But when the Gospel went out all through history, all those who truly become saved became part of the Bride of Christ.

This is great assurance to each one of us that we could never be put away. If we really became saved and if God granted us a new heart and a new spirit, no sin, not Vashti’s sin of disobedience, not any sin can ever be a reason for God to put us away. He has given us great assurance.

This is why it says in Romans 8:38-39:

For I am persuaded, that neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor principalities, nor powers, nor things present, nor things to come, Nor height, nor depth, nor any other creature, shall be able to separate us from the love of God, which is in Christ Jesus our Lord.

This word for “separate” is the word “put asunder” that is in Matthew 19 where it says, “What therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder.”

So nothing can ever cause God to look on any one of His people or His whole people collectively and say like Vashti or like Israel, “I am going to put you away.”

2nd Question (continued from Question #1): My question is not that. I know that God allowed divorce there, but the king divorced Vashti and then he married Esther. I thought that even in the Old Testament you could divorce but even if you divorced you were not allowed to remarry.

Chris: I see what you are saying. Like today, if someone divorces, they are actually still married; but that was not the case. A man could put away his wife and she would not be his wife any longer I think under that law. But we also have to remember that he had a harem. Solomon had 700 wives and 300 concubines. These are types and figures of God because, spiritually, God marries a multitude of people, a huge host of people; and yet He views it as one marriage. That is why He allowed Solomon to multiply wives in order to paint this picture.

 

3rd Question: Do you believe that Mordecai penned the book of Esther?

Chris: I do not know who penned Esther. We know that God wrote it. We do find in Esther 9:32, which is the last entry into the Old Testament:

And the decree of Esther confirmed these matters of Purim; and it was written in the book.

That would be the Bible, but it does not say who wrote it in the book. They could have had a scribe of the palace write it and doing the actual physical writing. We say that Paul wrote Romans, but he did not write Romans. Tertius is the scribe who wrote Romans.

But it does not matter who took a pen in hand. Even if Mordecai dictated it, it was God dictating to him and we always come back to that.

 

4th Question: I asked a few people during the break about that last thing that you mentioned as far as the 23 days followed by the five months. You totally lost me on that.

Chris: Okay. Let us go over this. Ezra joined Haggai and Zechariah together. They were contemporaries. They lived at the same time. They were under the rule of Darius who was the king of the Medes and the Persians.

They were building the temple at an earlier time than Esther. Esther is coming much later, say around 400 B.C. They are rebuilding the temple just a couple of decades after coming out of Jerusalem, but I do not know if we can accurately say the date that the temple was completed. Let us say that it was around 515 B.C.

So what is taking place in Haggai and Zechariah is over one hundred years earlier. But the important thing is that in Haggai very exact dates are given. Look at Haggai 1:1:

In the second year of Darius the king, in the sixth month, in the first day of the month…

So this is 6/1 in Darius’ second year.

We will not read all of this, but look at verse 10. We read in Haggai 1:10-11:

Therefore the heaven over you is stayed from dew, and the earth is stayed from her fruit. And I called for a drought upon the land…

But then a change is going to take place because Haggai is going to prophesy and this stirs up Zerubbabel and Joshua the high priest. Then it says in Haggai 1:14:

And JEHOVAH stirred up the spirit of Zerubbabel the son of Shealtiel, governor of Judah, and the spirit of Joshua the son of Josedech, the high priest, and the spirit of all the remnant of the people; and they came and did work in the house of JEHOVAH of hosts, their God,

To work in the house of God, we have looked at this before. The house of God is a physical illustration. It was a physical parable, because the Bible speaks of a spiritual house. It says in 1 Peter 2:5:

Ye also, as lively stones, are built up a spiritual house, an holy priesthood, to offer up spiritual sacrifices…

This is all believers. We are all living stones. Whenever God saves someone, it is like He adds a stone to the house that He is building. When the house is complete, then the salvation program is over.

So God is building a house and here in Haggai they are rebuilding the temple. They are building the house of God. God begins to communicate with Haggai at a very terrible time when there is a famine. This reminds us of the beginning of the great tribulation during those 2300 evening/mornings.

Actually, the famine continued in the church throughout the whole 23 years. But then the Lord stirs up Zerubbabel and Joshua and they begin to work in all the remnant. That is done in Haggai 1:15:

In the four and twentieth day of the sixth month, in the second year of Darius the king.

It began on 6/1, where God began communicating to Haggai, which was not a pleasant time. On 6/24, the second year of Darius, they are stirred up. They are working on the temple, the house of God.

Now look at the 23-year great tribulation where virtually no one is saved for 2300 evening/mornings. The latter rain begins to be poured out. Another way of looking at it is that the two witnesses stand on their feet. Their spirit was stirred and God’s people began to evangelize the world.

The ingredients for the great tribulation are found in Haggai 1 between the space of 23 days. How do we know that it was 23 days? 6/24 minus 6/1 and we get 23 days. At the end of 23 days, what day is that on? That would be 6/24.

By the way, this is just something that is interesting, but what day was the wall finished in the days of Nehemiah? It was finished in 52 days, but on what date was it finished?

In Nehemiah 6:15, it tells us:

So the wall was finished in the twenty and fifth day of the month Elul, in fifty and two days.

Elul is the sixth month. It was finished in the 25th day of the sixth month.

Back in Haggai, we have 24 days. We have day one where it is describing an awful period. Then God stirs up the spirit of His people. They begin to work, and then we are left at the end of chapter one on the 24th day of the 6th month. It so happens that in the book of Nehemiah the wall was completed on the 25th day of the 6th month.

In other words, if we would go to the next day, that would be the end of 23 days and we would be on the 25th day of the 6th month in Haggai. And what happened after 23 years? The wall of salvation, we understood, was finished. It was completed. This is just an additional thing to confuse us at this time.

But in Haggai 1, we have 6/1 when he begins to prophesy. On 6/24, 23 days later, turn to Zechariah and look at Zechariah 1:7:

Upon the four and twentieth day [or the 24th day] of the eleventh month, which is the month Sebat, in the second year of Darius…

It is the same year and the same king, and Ezra joins Haggai and Zechariah together. They both prophesied at the same time.

So 6/24 to 11/24 is how long? It is five months. It is like saying May 21 to October 21. We can easily see this because it ends on the same day of the month five months later. We know that May is our fifth month and October is our tenth month and it just goes five months.

Well, the 6th month and 24th day to the 11th month and 24th day of the Hebrew calendar is five months. This is the significance. We have 23 days in Haggai 1 ending on a date that is picked up in Zechariah, his contemporary, five months later, and then we read of Hadassah or hadac (had-as), Esther’s name or the myrtle tree. Then we get that strange vision.

I hope this helps.

 

5th Question: Can you explain more about October 21 as far as what you think happened spiritually and how it links into the five months and where we are now?

Chris: I do not know if I am fully ready to do this, but let us look at a verse. I think this might help, but I do not really understand it.

In Revelation 9, we read of five months. It says in Revelation 9:12:

One woe is past; and, behold, there come two woes more hereafter.

I taught this and Mr. Camping taught this. We believed that all three woes were simultaneous. It could be that the five months is one woe past. The word “hereafter” means “from now”:

…there come two woes more [from now].

The interesting thing is what we read in Revelation 9:13:

And the sixth angel sounded, and I heard a voice from the four horns of the golden altar which is before God,

And then it goes on to speak of those four messengers who were bound in the great river Euphrates and then their loosing. But after it mentions one woe is past, we read of four horns, and in Zechariah 1 we read of four horns after the 24th day of the 11th month, if you read through the whole chapter. Then there are four carpenters and it mentions four horns.

So this would seem to indicate that it is pointing to a period of time after five months that we missed.
