2nd Question: How does Haggai 2 fit in with the 23 days and the five months, because there is work going on and there is the shaking?

Chris: Look at Haggai 1, because in Zechariah God is linking the verses that we are reading in Zechariah 1 following the fifteenth verse of Haggai 1 because of the 5-month gap.

Remember that God can have multiple pictures and ongoing pictures. They still had to build the temple. They still had work to do. But in this case, there are 23 days of famine followed by the stirring up of Zerubbabel and Joshua. Then that chapter ends after 23 days on the 24th day of the 6th month; and then five months later, we come to Zechariah 1.

 

3rd Question: I am wondering about Genesis 8 and the 27th day of the 2nd month when all went out of the ark. This would be in May 2012. I know that we are looking at Purim, but have you thought about this, too?

Chris: We are trying to look at everything and the end of the ark or the historical end of the whole flood event happened in Genesis 8:13-14:

And it came to pass in the six hundredth and first year, in the first month, the first day of the month, the waters were dried up from off the earth: and Noah removed the covering of the ark, and looked, and, behold, the face of the ground was dry. And in the second month, on the seven and twentieth day of the month, was the earth dried.

A lot of the time, we have looked at this and we have thought of 370 days. This is because we know that the flood began the 17th day of the 2nd month and this is over a year later on the 27th day of the 2nd month. We think that it was 30-day months and we add 10 days from 17 to 27 and we say that it was 370 days, but not necessarily. This calendar relates to the Hebrew calendar.

Mike Cook did an interesting analysis of this and he demonstrated how this could have been exactly one year later, even though it seems to be several days over a year; and so there is a possibility.

I would say that if Purim were not the fulfillment of what God is looking at, this would probably be the last possibility before we would have to say that we were way off on something and would have to go back to the drawing board.

 

4th Question: Do we identify in our current situation with Nehemiah 2:3?

Chris: Nehemiah 2:3 says:

And said unto the king, Let the king live for ever: why should not my countenance be sad, when the city, the place of my fathers' sepulchres, lieth waste, and the gates thereof are consumed with fire?

No; I do not think that this is the picture.

Remember that Nehemiah was about to make a request to build the wall. That wall would take 52 days to build and the Bible speaks of walls of salvation. So this had everything to with beginning the project to build the spiritual wall – this is the way that we could look at this – within a period of 52 days, which relates to the “acceptable year of the Lord.”

That is not our situation. We are not looking to build or to bring people into the Kingdom or for God to evangelize the world and save all of His elect. We are not in that situation right now.

 

5th Question: I am a little concerned in regards to the Mayan calendar. How can it be that we could fall in line in exactly the same year as this?

Chris: I thought of the same thing and I understand what you are asking. Let us say that Christ returns in the year 2012 and that also happens to be the year that the Mayan calendar comes to an end. Well, that would not have anything to do with that.

I think that the Mayan calendar ends in December of 2012. As far as I know, that calendar is not even discussing the end of the world. That is just what people have derived from it.

God has His own plan and His own purposes, and so I just do not think that it plays any kind of part in this. It does not really come into view at all.

 

6th Question: I wanted to ask you about water in the Bible. We see God destroying the world with water and then we see God making water come from a rock to provide life, I guess, to some degree. Then we see God taking the Israelites through the Red Sea. Does “water” mean different things in different places in the Bible, or does it have a general meaning?

Chris: That is a good question. This is why we have to be careful when we are reading the Bible that we do not come up with a definition for a word or for an idea and apply it every time we find it.

For instance, the flood waters represent God’s judgment, but also the Word of God. The “water out of the rock” represents the Gospel going forth. As Moses struck the rock, Moses is a figure of the law of God. When Christ died for the sins of His people, Christ was struck by the law. Because of this, the Gospel could go out into the world.

So in every case, and there are many other places because we read about water everywhere in the Bible, we look at the context. We look at how it is being used.

For instance, the Bible tells us that “the wicked are like the troubled sea, when it cannot rest, whose waters cast up mire and dirt.” Here God is typifying the unsaved people of the world as a troubled sea, a wavy sea. But then the Bible also speaks of calm waters; and so we have to look at each place where that word is found.

 

7th Question: As I have been reading and looking back in the Old Testament about the crossing over of the Jordan, I know that this happens in the first month of the year. Maybe I ought to mention that it just keeps coming up to me that maybe that extra month might be something related to 13 or the super fullness. I do not know if there is any way that this could tie in; but if the end were actually to occur at the time of the Passover, then that would really tie into the crossing over into the Promised Land.

Chris: I do not think so, because the Passover identifies with Christ on the cross and giving Himself as the Lamb of God and the Passover was fulfilled at that point; but thanks because this gives me an opportunity to talk about this.

There were three feasts. One was the Passover, which was fulfilled spiritually when Jesus went to the cross, because He is “the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world.”

The second feast was the Feast of Pentecost. Remember that Acts 2 says “when the day of Pentecost was fully come.” This was when the Holy Spirit was poured out.

Was the Feast of Pentecost fulfilled on that day in 33 A.D.? No; it was an ongoing fulfillment. This is because throughout the whole church age, the firstfruits were brought in and Revelation speaks of 144,000 who are identified as firstfruits. It was like the kick-off in Acts 2, and then the whole firstfruits of the church age were brought in; and so the fulfillment of that second feast was of an ongoing nature over a period of 1,955 years.

The third feast is the Feast of Tabernacles. How is the Feast of Tabernacles to be fulfilled? What fulfills the Feast of Tabernacles? We thought, obviously, the end of the world; and, obviously, we were wrong. Something else fulfills the Feast of Tabernacles.

Let us go to Zechariah 14 and we will see here how God speaks of this feast. We read in Zechariah 14:16:

And it shall come to pass, that every one that is left of all the nations which came against Jerusalem shall even go up from year to year to worship the King, JEHOVAH of hosts, and to keep the feast of tabernacles.

You keep the feast “from year to year.” This always bothered me. Why do you keep this feast “from year to year”? If the keeping of this feast was fulfilled on the last day, why do you have to go up and keep it “from year to year”?

Let us go on. It says in Zechariah 14:17:

And it shall be, that whoso will not come up of all the families of the earth unto Jerusalem to worship the King, JEHOVAH of hosts, even upon them shall be no rain.

Let us think about the great tribulation of 23 years. For the first 2300 evening/mornings, there was no rain. Virtually, no one was being saved in the world. God says that this was necessary and then after that is when the sanctuary was to be cleansed. We read about this in Daniel 8.

Remember that Mr. Camping did a very good study of explaining what this meant. After that 2300-day period, which ended in September of 1994, the sanctuary was cleansed in the sense that from that point everyone who became saved would go right up. They would go right into the Kingdom of God.

It was not a gathering anymore, because God had already ended the church age, even though we did not know this. So this was not a gathering into the churches to collect the believers anymore. God was working one-on-one with individuals during the latter rain period when He saved them. When you become saved, you are seated “in heavenly places in Christ Jesus.” You go up. According to Ephesians, you go up to be with the Lord.

If you do not go up, if you are not saved, you experience no rain. Notice Zechariah 14:18:

And if the family of Egypt go not up…

Who became Egypt during the great tribulation? It was the church according to Revelation 11. Spiritually, God identified the church as Egypt and Sodom “where also our Lord was crucified,” and He was actually crucified outside Jerusalem.

It continues:

And if the family of Egypt go not up, and come not, that have no rain; there shall be the plague, wherewith JEHOVAH will smite the heathen that come not up to keep the feast of tabernacles.

So, in other words, what God did with the fulfillment of the Feast of Pentecost was that it was of an ongoing nature beginning on the day of Pentecost in 33 A.D. and it coincided with the first outpouring of the Holy Spirit.

The fulfillment of the Feast of Tabernacles occurred in September of 1994 when God began the latter rain period and it would be an ongoing keeping of the feast, an ongoing feast as the rain would fall in the world.

The churches refused to listen to God. They remained where they were, and so they had no rain. This is why it said that you had to go up to keep the feast from year to year, because it was over 17 years that this particular feast was being held.

Remember again that Mr. Camping did a study identifying the Feast of Tabernacles with the feast of the Bible. This is because we were learning so much information over this period of time that the Scriptures were opening up.

How could we have kept the feast of the Bible when the Feast of Tabernacles was pointing to October 21 in 2011 on a particular day? Well, it was not.

We were keeping the feast of the Bible. We were observing the Feast of Tabernacles from the 1st day of the 7th month in September of 1994 throughout and until the latter rain ended. That was the keeping of this feast; and the last day of “that great day of the feast” Jesus cried out and said, “If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink” because the door was about to shut. So it was during that period of time.

In other words, we were incorrect in taking the date of October 21 and thinking that it was the fulfillment of the end of the world. Of course, this is why we are still here.

The Feast of Tabernacles was not meant to be fulfilled by Christ coming on the very last day of the earth’s existence. But like the Feast of Pentecost, it was meant to be an ongoing feast over a period of time while God accomplished His purposes.

9th Question: Could you take a look at Acts 5:38-39? Does this tie into our day?

Chris: We read in Acts 5:38-39:

And now I say unto you, Refrain from these men, and let them alone: for if this counsel or this work be of men, it will come to nought: But if it be of God, ye cannot overthrow it; lest haply ye be found even to fight against God.

This relates to any group, but we have to consider who is saying this. It was Gamaliel who stood up and said this. He was a Pharisee. There was a big upheaval amongst the Pharisees concerning the Christians, and so he was actually speaking some words of calm. He calms the people down so that they do not continue to harass and kill these men. He says, “Just let them alone. If it is of God, then you cannot do anything about it. If it is of men, it will come to nought.”

This is true of anything. What God does is forever and what men do eventually fades away. That is the vanity of this world and of false religions and gospels also.

 

10th Question: In Daniel 4, we read about Nebuchadnezzar. There is a dream that Daniel interpreted for him where he was going to lose his kingdom. Later, he gave glory to God and then he regained that kingdom. Does this mean that Satan could still be ruling in the churches as of October 21?

Chris: No; it does not mean that. I know what you are referring to. A lot of people wonder about Nebuchadnezzar because of what he says in Daniel 4:34:

And at the end of the days I Nebuchadnezzar lifted up mine eyes unto heaven, and mine understanding returned unto me, and I blessed the most High, and I praised and honoured him that liveth for ever, whose dominion is an everlasting dominion, and his kingdom is from generation to generation:

We know that he was not a saved man, that he never became a saved man, and that he was a type of Satan.

It is true that in this historical context, he was restored to his kingdom; but this would be about the only place where a picture of Satan is restored; and I do not think that this relates to the day that we are living in.

What this does relate to in some ways is Haman. In Haman’s pride, he went into the king thinking, “Who would the king delight to honor more than me?” But then the king said, “Okay; go and honor Mordecai the Jew.”

So then there was Haman taking Mordecai on horseback through the streets; and what was Haman saying? He was saying, “Thus shall be done unto the man whom the king delights to honor.” He was speaking good things about Mordecai and good things about the king, because he was forced to.

This is all part of the judgment of God; and so here, too, as far as this relates to Satan, it would have that kind of aspect to it.

13th Question: In Zechariah, you were talking about the rest. What kind of rest did you have in view?

Chris: Well, it is not me. That word “rest” has to do with the cessation of war. For the land of Canaan, the conquest was complete; and then there was rest.

It is the same word in the book of Judges when Eglon, who had taken Israel captive in a sense, for 18 years. This reminds us of the woman in bondage for 18 years to Satan that Luke talks about. When Eglon was killed, the land had rest.

When Sisera was killed in the book of Judges by Jael, the land had rest. Again, when Athaliah was killed, there was quiet or rest. When Jehoshaphat and his army came back from the spoil of that great victory, there was rest in his days.

So, again and again and again, it means that the war is over, the battle is won, and now there is rest. It does not mean it physically on any kind of a level, because we, of course, are not going to see that in this world.
